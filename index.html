About Me

I am from Fort Wayne, Indiana and am studying computer science at the University of Notre Dame. This summer, I am working at Interactive Intelligence in Indianapolis, IN as a software engineering intern.

At Notre Dame, I am working hard to give back to the CS community that selflessly gave so much to me. Through serving as a teaching assistant for both of the introductory CS courses, I have been able to get to know the students in the class below me as well as help them kickstart their careers in CS, teach them the fundamentals, and offer advice from my own experiences whenever possible. Serving as the treasurer of the Notre Dame computer club has similarly allowed me to share experiences and advice with others in the CS program as well as plan and organize events to offer opportunities to others such as unique networking oppportunities and the ICPC programming competition.

Outside of coursework, I enjoy discovering and using new technologies in side projects. I have been actively working for Crcl, a startup from Notre Dame, since January 2016 and have thoroughly enjoyed the formidable challange of architecting and implementing their back-end. I recall that only a few short months before starting, I had never implemented a single line of server-side code before and felt mystified by the term "back-end development." However, after a few months of learning both by online resources and working with a few classmates on my first full-stack webapp, EatXpress, I began to thrive in the world of full-stack devleopment. I have the same eagerness today to continue to learn new technologies in order to solve bigger and more complex problems for society.

In my free time that I'm not working on a side project, I love to ride my bike, get out onto the tennis courts, and play video games with friends.
